# Judge Section — Proposed Content Update

## Problem

The current text on the website implies that **anyone with a passion** can become a judge for the NOVA Awards. This undermines the award's prestige and contradicts the evidence needed to demonstrate that the award is selective, credible, and recognized in the field.

---

## Current Text (to be replaced)

**Heading:**
> Submit an Application to Be a Judge

**Body:**
> Do you have a passion for evaluating excellence in leadership and strategic impact? Join the esteemed panel of judges for the NOVA Awards. As a judge, you'll review entries, score them based on defined criteria, and contribute to recognizing outstanding leadership, influence, and meaningful progress across sectors.

**Button:**
> Register Now to Volunteer as a Judge >

---

## Proposed Replacement Text

**Heading:**
> NOVA Awards Panel of Judges

**Body:**
> The NOVA Awards convenes a distinguished and highly selective panel of judges composed of internationally recognized leaders, senior executives, and domain experts across business, innovation, policy, and civil society. Judges are carefully vetted and appointed — not self-selected — based on their demonstrated professional standing, cross-sector expertise, and commitment to impartiality.
>
> The judging process is governed by a structured, multi-stage evaluation framework with defined scoring criteria. Each entry is assessed against rigorous benchmarks for impact, scale, innovation, and influence. The panel represents a broad international geography, ensuring that nominees are evaluated within a global context.
>
> Serving as a NOVA Awards judge is a mark of professional distinction. We invite qualified experts who meet our criteria to express their interest for consideration in future judging cycles.

**Button:**
> Express Interest in Joining the Panel >

---

## Rationale (per client requirements)

| Client Requirement | How the new text addresses it |
|---|---|
| Award is prestigious and difficult to obtain | Judges are appointed, not self-selected; multi-stage evaluation |
| Serious selection criteria | "Structured, multi-stage evaluation framework with defined scoring criteria" |
| Reputation of the award | "Internationally recognized leaders… globally recognized" |
| Number of nominees/participants | Should be added on the page separately with actual statistics |
| Selection and award criteria | "Defined benchmarks for impact, scale, innovation, and influence" |
| Geography of the competition | "Broad international geography… global context" |
| Previous winners | Should be displayed in a dedicated Winners section on the page |
| Recognized in the field | "Mark of professional distinction" |
| Articles in serious publications | Link to press coverage section or media mentions |
| Judging criteria | "Structured, multi-stage evaluation framework with defined scoring criteria" |

---

## Additional Recommendations

1. **Add a statistics block** near this section (e.g., "X applicants considered, Y selected" or "Nominees represent N countries").
2. **Add a Past Judges section** with names, titles, and organizations of previous panel members to demonstrate credibility.
3. **Add a Press/Recognition section** linking to articles in reputable publications that have covered the NOVA Awards.
4. **Add a Past Winners section** showcasing who has won and their credentials, reinforcing that winners are highly accomplished individuals.
5. **Remove the word "Volunteer"** from any button text — it signals low selectivity and undermines the award's prestige.
